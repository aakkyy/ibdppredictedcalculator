1. Why instrument exists

Predicting your IB grade is tedious, and the numbers that matter (subject boundaries, IA weighting, TOK/EE bonus points) are scattered across PDFs and spreadsheets. instrument puts the arithmetic in one place.

The whole site is built around one idea: an IB student shouldn't have to keep opening a calculator to know where they stand.

2. What it is

A small, focused toolkit for IBDP and IBCP students. Five tools:

  • The calculator, which predicts your total from whatever marks you have so far
  • The boundaries page, which shows every subject's grade thresholds for every timezone
  • The focus timer, a Pomodoro timer that logs your minutes by subject
  • The to-do list, tagged by subject and priority
  • The leaderboard, which ranks users by total focus time

3. Independence

instrument is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by the International Baccalaureate Organization. It is a student project built to solve a problem the author had.

All boundaries are sourced from the public May 2025 IB grading report. If you find a discrepancy between this site and your teacher's figures, trust your teacher and please report the error.

section 3
04
4. Principles

Three rules shape what goes on the site and what doesn't:

  • No accounts required. Every tool except the leaderboard works without signing in.
  • No ads, no tracking. The site doesn't sell your data because the site doesn't have your data.
  • Honest output. If a prediction is shaky because too many inputs are missing, the calculator says so. No inflated confidence.
