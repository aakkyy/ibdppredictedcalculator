1. The calculator

Pick your programme (DP or CP), pick your year, pick your timezone if you know it. Then add each subject one at a time, enter the marks you have already sat, and choose a target grade.

For each subject you get a live readout showing what you need to score in remaining assessments to hit that target, plus a warning if the target is not mathematically possible.

At the end you get your predicted total, a university match list based on typical entry requirements, and a shareable link.

02
2. The timer

A focus timer built around the pomodoro method and deep-work blocks. Pick a subject, pick a length (25, 45, 60, or 90 minutes), start the clock.

Each completed session is logged to your browser and feeds a streak counter and a session history. Breaks are tracked separately so your focus minutes are honest.

03
3. The paper archive

A structured reference for past papers. Filter by subject, year, and session. Each paper entry shows what it contains and links to the official IB source where available.

You can mark papers as attempted or pending so you can see at a glance what still needs to be revised. Data stays on your device.

topic 3
04
4. The mock exam

A timed mock simulator for when full paper practice feels too much and you need exam-pressure practice under constraints.

Pick a subject and paper length. The timer runs down in full-screen mode. When it ends, enter your raw mark and get your grade against the current TZ boundaries, plus a distance-to-next-grade readout.

topic 4
05
5. The boundaries

A full reference of grade boundaries for all 63 subjects from the May 2025 IBDP grading report. 48 subjects have timezone-specific boundaries and will show a TZ1/TZ2/TZ3 badge. The rest show Default.

Search by subject name, switch timezones from the top, see grade ranges colour-coded for quick reading.

6. Weighting formula

For each subject in the calculator, your weighted percentage is:

  • SA1 = 10%
  • SA2 = 20%
  • SA3 = 20%
  • SA4 = 35%
  • IA = 15%

Your IA score (out of 20) is converted to a percentage before weighting. Missing assessments are treated as not yet sat and left out of the locked score.

07
7. IBCP mode

If you pick CP in setup, the calculator switches to Career-related Programme rules. You pick 2 to 4 DP subjects, there is no 3-HL requirement, and instead of TOK + EE you grade your Reflective Project A to E.

Your total is calculated out of your subject count multiplied by 7. The CP Certificate requirements are shown at the end with a check on your Reflective Project grade.

8. If something looks wrong

If a grade boundary looks off, a calculation disagrees with what your teacher said, or a subject is missing, flag it through the socials page. Include the subject name, the level, and the timezone if relevant.
